A mutual fund is a pooled investment vehicle managed by professionals who invest the pooled money into diversified portfolios of securities such as stocks, bonds, or money market instruments. Investors buy shares in the mutual fund, which represent their portion of the fund's holdings and its income. Mutual funds offer diversification, professional management, and liquidity, making them popular among individual investors looking to access diversified investments without directly managing them.
